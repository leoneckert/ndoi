Nomadic Department of the Interior

About

The Nomadic Department of the Interior (NDOI) is a research collective that seeks to address, in films, websites, and publications, the conflicting interests between stewardship of lands & extraction of material and cultural resources. Our research investigates technological systems against the backdrop of geological, ecological, human history.

Our current project is about the western Chinese province of Guizhou. This is part of a larger, long-term body of work about the collision of technology and rural places.

Our members are currently based in China and Taiwan.

Members

Ann Chen is an artist and filmmaker based in Brooklyn and Shanghai. She studied at the Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP) at New York University (NYU). As an inaugural recipient of the National Geographic Digital Storytelling Fulbright Fellowship, she researched and documented the proposed pathway for an oil pipeline in northwestern Canada.

Born and raised in Germany, Leon Eckert studied Design at Goldsmiths University and graduated from ITP at NYU. He contributed to AdNauseam.io and DataSelfie.it, and did research and development for artists Trevor Paglen and Adam Harvey. He is currently an instructor of Interactive Media Arts (IMA) at NYU Shanghai teaching net-art and critical data courses.

Yutong Lin graduated from IMA at NYU Shanghai. As a Nakhi from Yunnan, China, she is an activist for indigenous visibility and equality through ethnographic imaging and writing practices. Her graduation thesis explores how social media colonialism reforms the landscape and tourism infrastructure in her hometown.

Yufeng Zhao is an artist and technologist with a double-major in IMA and Computer Science from NYU Shanghai. He recently built an automation software for short video productions serving digital laborers on the Chinese platform Kuaishou and currently researches telepresence experiences.
